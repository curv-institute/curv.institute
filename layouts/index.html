WHAT WE DO

CURV Institute develops and tests Representational Science, a scientific framework that studies how complex systems encode, transform, and regulate information, and how failures at the representational level give rise to instability, inefficiency, and loss of control.

Representational Science introduces new, testable structure designed to remain consistent with established physics; consistency demonstrations to date are reduced-sector, model-system results (e.g., Schrödinger and weak-field recoveries in surrogate models), and the bridge from these reduced parameters to the framework's top-level multiplier is an open derivation problem. CURV Institute operationalizes this into reproducible diagnostics and control methods.
